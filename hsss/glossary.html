📖 Glossary of Key Terms – HSS F101

← Back to Index

Aadhaar: India’s biometric identity system; 1.38B enrolled.
Anthropocene: Proposed geological epoch defined by human impact on Earth.
Categorical Imperative: Kant’s unconditional moral command (universalizability, respect for persons).
Civil Disobedience: Nonviolent, public, conscientious law-breaking (Thoreau, Gandhi, MLK).
Collectivism: Cultural orientation where group goals precede individual goals (India).
Cultural Hegemony (Gramsci): Dominance achieved by making ruling-class values appear as common sense.
Deepfake: AI-generated fake images/videos; raises consent and misinformation concerns.
Dharma: Righteousness, moral duty; one of the Purusharthas.
Doctrine of Mean (Aristotle): Virtue is a mean between extremes (e.g., courage).
DPI (Digital Public Infrastructure): India’s model of open, interoperable digital rails (Aadhaar, UPI, DEPA).
Eudaimonia: Aristotle’s concept of human flourishing / good life.
Fake News: Fabricated information; includes misinformation, disinformation, malinformation.
Fundamental Attribution Error: Overestimating internal causes for others’ behaviour.
General Will (Rousseau): Collective will of the people; basis of legitimate law.
Group Polarization: Group discussion strengthens initial attitudes, leading to extremes.
Groupthink: Faulty decision-making due to pressure for unanimity.
Hegemony: Dominance through consent and cultural leadership (Gramsci).
High‑context communication: Meaning conveyed implicitly through context (India).
Ideology: System of ideas that shapes reality and justifies power relations.
Interpellation (Althusser): Process by which ideology “hails” individuals into subjects.
Legitimacy (Weber): Traditional, charismatic, legal‑rational authority.
Media Ethics: Principles guiding truthful, fair, and respectful communication.
Milgram Experiment: Study showing high obedience to authority, even when harming others.
Political Obligation: Moral/legal duty to obey state laws.
Polychronic time: Time is fluid, relationships prioritized over schedules (India).
Prejudice: Preconceived negative attitude towards an out‑group.
Purusharthas: Four aims of life: Dharma, Artha, Kama, Moksha.
Ritual (Victor Turner): Stereotyped sequence to influence supernatural forces.
Separation of Powers: Division of government into Legislature, Executive, Judiciary (Montesquieu).
Social Contract: Agreement among individuals to form state (Hobbes, Locke, Rousseau).
Social Movement: Collective effort to promote/resist social change.
UPI (Unified Payments Interface): India’s real‑time payment system; 13.4B transactions/month.
Utilitarianism: Ethical theory maximizing overall happiness (Bentham, Mill).
Virtue Ethics: Focus on character and virtues for a good life (Aristotle).
Visual Culture: Production, consumption, and interpretation of images.
Whistle‑blowing: Moral protest by releasing nonpublic information about wrongdoing.
